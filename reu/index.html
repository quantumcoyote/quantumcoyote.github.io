Confined Spaces in Materials Science (CSMS)

Separations and Catalysis

Adsorption and chemical reactions on a surface are central to materials science and to many industrial processes. An interaction of a guest (generally a molecule or an ion) inside a nanopore is analogous to a surface interaction but introduces a confinement effect. The size and chemical functionalization of the nanopore can be tuned to bind a guest stronger or weaker, to encourage a guest to interact at a particular binding site, or template the packing of guests within the nanopore. Understanding the molecular-level details within the nanopore can be challenging to characterize both experimentally and computationally. All of the proposed research projects focus on using the expertise of each group to study an application in separations chemistry or catalysis within a confined space. USD's Chemistry and Biomedical Engineering departments will host six undergraduate REU students at the main campus in Vermillion, SD in an intensive 10 week summer research experience with USD faculty mentors involving projects related to separations or catalysis in confined spaces.

CSMS Program in a Nutshell

The CSMS program will host six undergraduate REU students each summer in an intensive 10 week summer research experience with USD chemistry and biomedical engineering faculty mentors involving projects related to separations or catalysis in confined spaces. The participants in the CSMS program will have a research stipend, free on campus housing for the duration of the program, a food allowance among other benefits. Furthermore they will visit the South Dakota state capital to present their research as well as and enjoy summer in the Mount Rushmore state.

Research Stipend

Each undergraduate student participant in the program will recive a $500/week reseach stipend, totalling $5000 for the ten week summer REU program.

On Campus Housing

Free ten week on campus housing at the USD Coyote Village which provides fully furnished acomodations including a common area with a 42'' HD television, fridge, microwave, couches, and coffee tables.

Food Allowance

A $700 food allowance will be provided in a USD Coyote card which can be used for dinning on and off-campus, including Vermillion's grocery stores and restaurants.

Present Your Research

In the last week of the REU program all undergraduate students will travel to the South Dakota EPSCoR Undergraduate Research Symposium in Pierre, SD to present a scientific poster with their research.

Scientific Seminars

Attend a series of seminars and workshops on state-of-the-art scientific instrumentation and computational methods, scientific communication skills and future career paths.

Visit South Dakota

Enjoy the summer in South Dakota. CSMS program will organize summer BBQs, a visit Gavin's Point Dam and the National Fish Hatchery, a trip to a Sioux Falls Canaries baseball game and a canoe trip down a portion of the Missouri river.
