Undergraduate Research

The Miro and Vlaisavljevich groups are searching for USD science students (freshman to senior) interested in performing undergraduate research in their groups. We have multiple positions available during Fall and Spring semesters as well as during summer. Students can either perform voluntary or for credit research. Funding for summer research stipends may be available.

Learn Modern Quantum Chemistry

Learn computational chemistry methods applied to energy, catalysis, and environmental problems. The Miro and Vlaisavljevich groups have over 10 years of experience using state-of-the-art computational methodologies.

Use a Supercomputer

Use Lawrence, the new University of South Dakota supercomputer to deal with all the math and equations for you. Over a hundred nodes with the latest INTEL processors (two SkyLake Xeon with 16 cores per node) and 192 GB of memory. Learn how to use quantum mechanical software packages to solve the chemistry questions.

Analyze Your Calculations

Make fundamental predictions of electronic strucutre, catalytic reactions, chemical bonds, and molecular properties. Visualize molecular orbitals, obtain spectra (e.g. IR, UV-Vis, NMR...), identify key intermediates (e.g. transition states), calculate reaction constants, understand electron interactions and much more.

Get Research Experience

Perform cutting edge scientific research to complement your academic training. Undergraduate research is a key factor in applications to graduate and professional schools (Medical School, Pharmacy, Dental Schools...), and adds to your CV/Resume.

How to Apply

If you are interested in joining either of groups, send a short paragraph describing your research interests including the area of your major/minor to:

 

Dr. Pere Miro (pere.miro@usd.edu)

Dr. Bess Vlaisavljevich (bess.vlaisavljevich@usd.edu)
